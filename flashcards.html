What are the primary air pollutants of concern for human health?
Click to flip
2
The six primary air pollutants (criteria pollutants) are: particulate matter (PM), ground-level ozone, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, nitrogen dioxide, and lead. These pollutants can cause respiratory problems, cardiovascular issues, and other serious health effects, especially in vulnerable populations.
